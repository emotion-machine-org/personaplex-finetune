# Model Card — PersonaPlex Finetune (Voice Training Workspace)

This card describes the *finetuning recipe and trained adapters* produced
by this repository. Specific released checkpoints (when published) will
each have their own card linking back here for the recipe.

## Model details

- **Model type:** Voice-native streaming language model (text + 8-codebook
  agent audio + 8-codebook user audio at 12.5 Hz, 24 kHz Mimi codec).
- **Base models:**
  - [`nvidia/personaplex-7b-v1`](https://huggingface.co/nvidia/personaplex-7b-v1)
    — 7B params, `dep_q=16`, voice + role conditioning over Moshi.
  - [`kyutai/moshiko-pytorch-bf16`](https://huggingface.co/kyutai/moshiko-pytorch-bf16)
    — base Moshi (PersonaPlex's parent).
- **Adapter:** LoRA (rank 32–128, scaling 2.0). ~5.6% of params trainable.
- **Languages:** English. Other languages untested (Moshi is English-only
  out of the box).
- **License:** Adapter weights released under the NVIDIA Open Model
  License (inherited from PersonaPlex). Code in this repo is MIT.

## Intended use

- Research on voice-native conversational models.
- Building domain-specific voice agents (the included recipes target
  *companion chat* and *pharma patient support* with mid-conversation
  context injection).
- Reproducing or extending the experiments documented in
  [`docs/history/notes/combined_experiment_report.md`](docs/history/notes/combined_experiment_report.md).

## Out of scope

- Real-time customer-facing deployment without human review of outputs —
  duplex models hallucinate; PersonaPlex is no exception.
- Cloning a specific person's voice without their consent.
- Languages other than English (no eval coverage).
- Safety-critical or regulated domains (medical advice, legal advice,
  financial advice) — the model is not aligned for these.

## Training procedure

- **Hardware:** 5× 24 GB GPUs (typical), FSDP. Single-GPU 24 GB OOMs
  during optimizer state allocation.
- **Optimizer:** AdamW, weight decay 0.01.
- **Learning rate:** 1e-6 to 2e-6 (companion stable boundary 2e-6;
  pharma stable boundary 1e-6 — see report Section 4).
- **Schedule:** Linear warmup, no decay (short runs: 64–1024 steps).
- **Loss:** Standard NLL on text + 8 audio codebooks. Two stability fixes
  documented in the report:
  1. `text_padding_weight=0.0` (or 0.03 for pharma) — eliminates PAD
     token collapse.
  2. L2 regularization on `lora_B` weights (1e-4) — prevents catastrophic
     forgetting.
- **NEFTune** noise (alpha=5) optional, used in some pharma runs.
- **Context injection (puppeteer):** for pharma — text loss only on
  injected context tokens, no audio loss.

## Training data

Synthetic dialogues generated by Claude (LHS-sampled across persona
parameters), rendered to speech by [VibeVoice 7B](https://github.com/microsoft/VibeVoice),
aligned with WhisperX. Per-sample sidecar JSON carries `text_prompt`,
`voice_prompt`, and (for pharma) `context_injections` with frame offsets.

- **Companion v6:** 1,000 synthetic conversations + 564 curated podcast
  clips (English).
- **Pharma:** 2,003 samples, mean duration 296 s, structured
  patient-support call format.

Training data is **not redistributed** with this repo (it's gigabytes and
its license depends on which TTS / podcast sources you use). The pipeline
in `pipeline/` regenerates equivalent data from public sources.

See [`data/README.md`](data/README.md) for the dataset card.

## Evaluation

- **Generation eval (during training):** every N steps the model
  conditions on held-out prompts and generates 30 s of speech; transcripts
  are scored 1–5 on naturalness, accuracy, and (for pharma) script
  adherence by a Claude / Gemini judge.
- **Batch eval (post-training):** larger held-out set, same judges.
- **A/B preference** (companion only): pairwise human preference between
  two checkpoints via the `personaplex/client/` UI.

Results, including failed runs, are in
[`docs/history/notes/combined_experiment_report.md`](docs/history/notes/combined_experiment_report.md).
Per-run config + full unsampled metric history (parquet) for every
historical run is in [`docs/history/wandb/`](docs/history/wandb/).

## Bias, risks, limitations

- Training data is synthetic, English-only, and biased toward American
  English speakers (VibeVoice voice library). Outputs reflect this.
- Synthetic dialogues are fluent but stylized; the model may default to a
  characteristic "synthetic" cadence on out-of-distribution prompts.
- No red-team or jailbreak evaluation. Do not deploy in adversarial
  contexts.
- Voice cloning capability: PersonaPlex can be voice-conditioned. We do
  not release a voice-cloning preset; users who do should follow consent
  norms.

## Environmental impact

A typical run is 5×3090 (~1100 W total) for 4–24 hours. Total compute
across the experiments documented in the report is roughly 600 GPU-hours
on consumer GPUs. We did not formally measure carbon impact.
